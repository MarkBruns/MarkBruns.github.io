# Scriptural basis for "Iran will bless Israel" prophesy

Current events (e.g., Iranian diaspora, reported Christian growth in Iran, or political shifts) reassure some of us believers to see Jeremiah 49 unfolding and to pray for Iranians under the Genesis 12:3 principle ("I will bless those who bless you [Israel and/or the Jewish people], and him who dishonors you I will curse, and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed"). 

But these are old applications stating that "Iran will bless Israel" (or ancient Persia/Elam doing so) in the future and primarily are reassuring HOPES, certainly not brand new prophecies. 

The Bible consistently shows God’s faithfulness to Israel regardless of surrounding nations’ actions. For deeper study, the passages above (especially in context) are the scriptural foundation.

### 1. Historical Blessing from Persia (Not a Future Prophecy)
Ancient Persia (modern Iran) **did** bless Israel under King Cyrus the Great:

- **Isaiah 44:28–45:1, 13** (written ~150 years before Cyrus): God calls Cyrus (king of Persia) His “shepherd” and “anointed” who will say of Jerusalem, “She shall be rebuilt,” and of the temple, “Your foundation shall be laid.” God says He will open doors before Cyrus, subdue nations, and use him to free the Jewish exiles and fund the temple’s rebuilding.  

  This was fulfilled in **Ezra 1:1–4** and **2 Chronicles 36:22–23**, when Cyrus decreed the return from Babylonian exile and provided resources. Persia protected Jews further in the books of Esther and Nehemiah.

These are **fulfilled history**, not a prediction of future Iran. Some teachers draw parallels to modern events (e.g., hoping for a “Cyrus-like” shift in Iran), but Scripture presents this as past fulfillment.

### 2. General Principle (Applies to Any Nation or Person)
- **Genesis 12:3** (God’s promise to Abraham/Israel): “I will bless those who bless you, and him who dishonors you I will curse, and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed.”

This is **not** a prophecy that Iran *specifically* will bless Israel. It is a timeless principle: God blesses individuals or nations that treat Israel kindly (seen historically in Persia’s case). Several teachers reference it alongside current Iran-Israel tensions, but it does in any way not predict Iran’s actions.

### 3. Prophecy of Judgment + Later Restoration for Elam (Part of Ancient Iran)
The closest verse some interpret positively for Iran’s *people* (not the regime) is in Jeremiah:

- **Jeremiah 49:34–39** (prophecy against Elam, an ancient region/administrative center in what is now Iran):  
  “Thus says the Lord of hosts: ‘Behold, I will break the bow of Elam, the mainstay of their might… And I will bring upon Elam the four winds from the four quarters of heaven, and I will scatter them to all those winds… I will terrify Elam before their enemies… I will send the sword after them, until I have consumed them, and I will set my throne in Elam and destroy their king and officials… **But in the latter days I will restore the fortunes of Elam**, declares the Lord.’”

  - The judgment/scattering parts are seen by some (including Messianic Jewish Iranian believers) as partially fulfilled today: Iranian diaspora worldwide, military setbacks, and regime pressures.  
  - “Set my throne in Elam” and “restore the fortunes” in the latter days are often interpreted as a future spiritual revival (e.g., millions of Iranian Muslims reportedly turning to Christ, mosques closing, bold underground church growth). This is **not** described as Iran blessing Israel—it is about God’s sovereignty and restoration for Elam’s people after judgment. No link to Israel support is stated in the text.

### 4. Explicit End-Times Prophecy: Persia *Against* Israel
The clearest prophecy involving Persia/Iran in the future is negative:
- **Ezekiel 38:1–6, 39:1–7** (Gog/Magog coalition, widely seen as latter-days/end-times event):  
  “Persia, Cush, and Put are with them, all of them with shield and helmet… You will come up against my people Israel, like a cloud covering the land.” God supernaturally destroys the coalition on the mountains of Israel so that “the nations will know that I am the Lord, the Holy One in Israel.”

  Persia (modern Iran) is listed as an ally in an invasion of Israel. God intervenes dramatically; the invaders are defeated. This is the dominant end-times reference to Iran in prophecy teaching. It foreshadows opposition, not blessing.

### Summary

- **No verse** says Iran (or Persia/Elam) “will bless Israel” as a nation or in the end times.

- Positive elements are **historical** (Cyrus) or **general** (Genesis 12:3) or limited to **restoration after judgment** for Elam’s people (Jeremiah 49:39—often tied to revival, not geopolitical blessing of Israel).

- The forward-looking prophecy (Ezekiel 38–39) places Persia in opposition, defeated by God.
