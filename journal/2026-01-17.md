# Not JUST Polymarket: 100 Prediction Market Alternatives

1. Kalshi
- Offers federally regulated event contracts on real-world outcomes like economics, politics, and sports, providing users with accurate crowd-sourced forecasts and hedging opportunities in a compliant U.S. environment.
- Free to sign up; trades in USD with minimum deposits starting at $5; U.S. residents only, requires identity verification for access.
- Highly regulated nature limits market variety to approved events, potentially reducing excitement for niche or global topics; resolution can be slow due to CFTC oversight.
- https://kalshi.com

2. Augur
- Decentralized Ethereum-based platform allowing users to create and trade on any event outcome, empowering peer-to-peer forecasting without central intermediaries for true market-driven predictions.
- Uses ETH for trades; no minimum, but gas fees apply; open to anyone with an Ethereum wallet, though defunct since 2022.
- Platform is now defunct, leading to no active trading or liquidity; historical issues with high fees and slow resolutions made it unreliable for timely events.
- https://augur.net (archived)

3. Good Judgment Open
- Reputation-based forecasting site where users earn points for accurate predictions on global events, fostering collaborative wisdom-of-crowds insights without financial risk.
- Free access; no monetary betting, only reputation scores; open to all with an account signup.
- Lacks real financial incentives, making it less engaging for profit-seekers; predictions are subjective and not always timely or comprehensive.
- https://goodjudgmentopen.com

4. Iowa Electronic Markets
- Academic platform for low-stakes betting on elections and economic events, providing educational value and real-money experience with capped risks.
- $5 to $500 investment limit; U.S. residents primarily, requires account approval; educational/academic access.
- Strict limits on stakes reduce appeal for serious traders; focused only on specific events like elections, limiting variety.
- https://iemweb.biz.uiowa.edu

5. Manifold
- Play-money prediction market where users bet virtual currency on diverse topics, encouraging fun, low-risk experimentation and community-driven questions.
- Free; uses virtual "Mana" currency; open to all with signup.
- No real-money payouts, so lacks financial motivation; markets can be manipulated by coordinated groups due to low barriers.
- https://manifold.markets

6. Metaculus
- Reputation-based site for numeric and date-range forecasts on science, tech, and global events, ideal for detailed, quantitative predictions and expert collaboration.
- Free; no monetary aspect; open to all.
- Predictions are non-financial, reducing stakes; community bias can skew results on controversial topics.
- https://metaculus.com
