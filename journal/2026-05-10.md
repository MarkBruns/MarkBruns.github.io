# Happy Mother's Day

My mother was a RUTHlessly practical tough woman; I really do believe that she was much tougher than anybody else I know ... incredibly kind and gracious in a superficial way, but also deep down, she could be MEAN as total livingF if she was ever crossed. I am mostly my father's son, apple falling from the tree-wise, but my Dad was never remotely as MEAN as my dear mother was ... and he also did not have her capacity for kindness and gentleness, at least to those who didn't know him, ie my Dad might have been known for his gentleness, but he was the kind of officer who ran a group of assassins working for the occupation forces on the East German border or often inside East Germany -- so I'm kind of like that, completely gentle and kind on the surface, but also meanAF; I truly enjoy dicey situations in which [bad] people get hurt and I have killed a couple of nasty people [on the other side of the border].

I am not one bit a church person; don't care AT ALL for church services ... I think that christians who show up in a church on sunday to be seen as christians are total morons and herdable sheep ... but I think Sunday School, catechism, daily devotions and bible study are important, largely because of my mother's example.  My mother was a Sunday School teacher who patiently taught little kids about Jesus and had absolutely no time whatsoever for telling lies to children with bullshit about Santa Claus or the Easter bunny. My mother was not given to violence, in the way that I am, but I know that she'd want me to smack the livingF out of any dufus pushing Santa or Easter Bunny shit around kids. I am deeply disturbed by teachers, with access to kids, who willfully spread lies about bullshit like Santa or some stupid rodent-like creature dispensing candy eggs. No wonder schools are EVIL, when the so-called professionals in schools start lying to kids the first time that you interact with them!  My mother was subject to worries and fears, so there were rather obvious and glaring faults which were a blessing in that her example taught me positive things, but also taught me the importance of NEVER really trusting anyone who gives in to worries or lets any form of fear drives their lives.

With my mother in mind, I am contemplating what I should be working on in the coming decades of my life.  I asked Grok to give me a starter list for new frontiers that I should be pushing into. I will use the list of 200 ideas to find five that I should probably be working on.  The first step is to examine each idea and give each a rating ... basically, there will probably be about 50 ideas in each of the A, B, C, D categories, although with additional sorting, I'll probably end up 5 ideas to implement, 20 ideas to use as substitutes in case one of the five washes out, 50 second-string ideas, in case one of the first 25 wash out, 125 ideas for the back parking lot or Area 51 bin.

A) The top five ... practical, doable now or basically almost immediately

B) The next 20 ... almost, but the time is not right or something similar; the idea's good, but needs one other thing to change

C) The 50 second-stringers ... might work, but would need some changes and heavier development than I see immediately useful

D) The 125 wierd-ass Area 51 ideas ... these are ok to provoke ideas and maybe could actually benefit the top five working ideas, but generally these should be relegated to the parking lot.
