# Transformational Discipleship For the Glory of God

We believers are the LIVING embodiment of the profound, continuing biblical transition—beginning with Jesus’ direct, earthly leadership over His disciples (the “sheep”) to the post-resurrection, Spirit-empowered era where Peter receives a specific foundational commission, and onward through the ages in which ALL believers are called **by Christ Himself** to ACTIVE, personal advocacy through the Holy Spirit.

We must emphasize that the church was never simply handed over to ONE member of the flock to dominate the others OR to build artificial human structures for pressure. Our accountability is to the Holy Triune God alone.

### 1. Christ Did Not Turn the Church Over To a Sheep-like Person; Leadership Was Entrusted to Peter

Jesus consistently portrayed believers, particularly new believers, as sheep who benefit from shepherding (see John 10:11–16, where He calls Himself the Good Shepherd). After His resurrection, He specifically commissioned **Peter**—whom He had already named the “rock”—with the personality needed to fight for the flock.

- **Matthew 16:18–19** (Jesus speaking to Peter after his confession):  
  “And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades will not overcome it. I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven; whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.”

- **John 21:15–17** (post-resurrection reinstatement):  
  “When they had finished eating, Jesus said to Simon Peter, ‘Simon son of John, do you love me more than these?’ … Jesus said, ‘Feed my lambs.’ … ‘Take care of my sheep.’ … ‘Feed my sheep.’”  

We should note that Jesus did not say “Peter, bully my sheep!” or “Peter, make voting decisions for my sheep!” He said “Feed” and “Take care of” the sheep. This is a pastoral, nurturing role, not a political or authoritarian one.

### 2. Peter's Transformation Thru The Holy Spirit: “From Violent Defender TO Even More FEARLESS and Bold Apostle”

Peter was impulsive, passionate, and ready to defend Jesus’ “legacy” physically. The fact that Christ found Peter’s fiery, protective nature amusing is clear. Peter was the one who drew a sword and used it in the garden. Christ did not rebuke Peter for his passion or his willingness to defend Him. Instead, He rebuked Peter for using violence and then healed the servant’s ear (Luke 22:51). Christ told Peter to put away his sword, but He did not tell him to throw away his sword or to stop being passionate or to stop defending the flock. 

In fact, Christ’s understanding of the moment of passion [without condemning Peter or telling him that he was wrong to bear a sword] was a moment of transformation for Peter, in which he stopped being a violence-first defender of the flock. Peter's transformation continued as he became bold, aggressive, outspoken, very public advocate for Christ’s teachings.

Yet Peter, like all of us, was deeply, profoundly flawed—so his passion was brittle and fragile. But Christ loved, understood, and chose Peter. Scripture also shows his failures (denial of Christ three times in John 18) and his restoration in John 21. Thus, Peter illustrates one of the most profound miraculously transformative aspects of Christian faith: its core themes of resurrection, restoration, and redemption. 

We are to perfect ourselves through FAITH ALONE. Our faith is not to encourage meekness—we are not to follow perfect or falsely infallible leaders. Jesus gave us a very important lesson in how He chose this bold, flawed defender to shepherd the disciples **temporarily**, until the Advocate—the promised Helper arrived ... and when the Holy Spirit arrived, it's arguable that the most miraculous transformation happened in Peter and the complete removal of Peter's FEAR of what others thought.

Peter's EXAMPLE is also a question for us: Are we willing to ask God to give us the grace of letting go of our fears and insecurities and step into the role of being salt and light in the world, as Christ commanded ... or are we too worried about what others think, what our legacy might be, whether we should try harder to fit in as domineering voices in our culture of suicidal empathetic want us to be? Are we willing to be transformed by the Holy Spirit, to be bold advocates for Christ, even if it means facing criticism, rejection, persecution, torture or death? Are we willing to work at making ourselves stronger, more courageous, to be physically ready to exploit the opportunities for service that we might be given?  

### 3. “Until the Advocate or Holy Spirit Would Arrive” — Exactly Right
Jesus repeatedly told the disciples He would send “another Advocate” (Greek *Parakletos*, meaning Helper, Counselor, or one who stands beside in defense) after His departure:

- **John 14:16, 26**: “And I will ask the Father, and he will give you another advocate to help you and be with you forever… But the Advocate, the Holy Spirit, whom the Father will send in my name, will teach you all things and will remind you of everything I have said to you.”

- **John 15:26**: “When the Advocate comes, whom I will send to you from the Father—the Spirit of truth who goes out from the Father—he will testify about me.”

- **John 16:7**: “But very truly I tell you, it is for your good that I am going away. Unless I go away, the Advocate will not come to you; but if I go, I will send him to you.”

This “until” is fulfilled dramatically at **Pentecost** (Acts 2), when the Holy Spirit descends on **all** the gathered believers (not just Peter), filling them with power. Peter—once violent in person but fearful in front of crowds—now preaches BOLDLY to thousands. Thus, another lesson of Peter and the Holy Spirit is that we are to EXPECT dramatic transformations in our times, in our lives. We are not to look back, following the dated traditions of humans from previous millennia, even though, through the Holy Spirit, the words of Christ become eternally fresh and immediately transformative for us in the moments of today and every day.

### 4. “Not Sheep, but Staunch Advocates for Christ … to BE the SALT, or to BE the LIGHT”
With the Holy Spirit indwelling every believer, the role of advocacy and influence expands to the entire church—exactly as **Christ commanded**. As we are filled by the Holy Spirit, we are no longer supposed to be merely passive sheep looking for direction under one visible leader. As believers and loving practitioners of the faith in our daily lives, we are called by Christ to become part of a royal priesthood empowered to glorify God, to declare God’s praises, and to engage the world as individuals—not as mobs—and to spread the gospel. We are NOT called to force others to submit or to follow any human teachers. We are called **by Christ** to be salt and light, to influence through our example, through the “content of our character,” and through personal witness, not through coercion or political power.

- **Matthew 5:13** (Jesus’ own words): “You are the salt of the earth. But if the salt loses its saltiness, how can it be made salty again? It is no longer good for anything, except to be thrown out and trampled underfoot.”  
  (Salt preserves, flavors, and creates thirst—exactly the active, influential role Christ commands.)

- **Matthew 28:19–20** (the Great Commission, Christ’s direct command): “Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age.”

Being the SALT or the LIGHT, leading by EXAMPLE, is what faith in the Triune God (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit) is all about. We are not saved by works, but by grace through faith—but if we love others as we love ourselves, we must want to share our own testimony, to share our example, and necessarily to invite criticism. Criticism helps us to grow and mature in our faith—it does NOT mean that we always change based on criticism; sometimes criticism simply points out yet another aspect of our example that we must make more clear to those who fail to understand it. Every Christian is now called **by Christ** to be an advocate—living as salt and light. Filled with the Holy Spirit, we are NO LONGER TO BE JUST SHEEP!

We are richly blessed to be part of the human exchange of ideas and testimonies of faith that includes a shift to universal empowerment that Christ Himself inaugurated. The transformative power of the Holy Spirit continues through until the moments of our lives this very day, exactly as Jesus promised. While early leadership existed, Christ remains the sole Head and Chief Shepherd. We follow **His** commands directly through the living Holy Spirit, not through any artificial human hierarchies, canonized traditions, or self-appointed authorities that seek to dominate. 

The New Testament pattern is one body of Christ, one body of believers, but with many different and unique members, each empowered differently yet members of the same body, all continuing to be accountable to Jesus Christ the Head alone—and NOT to any artificial, self-selected charlatan at the head of a political lynch mob.

Jesus entrusted initial leadership to Peter the rock (the defender) until the Advocate arrived. Now, through the Triune God, every believer is called **by Christ** and, through the transformative power of the Holy Spirit, equipped to be a bold advocate—as salt preserving and flavoring the world. The Holy Spirit democratizes advocacy without erasing the need to reject every form of covetous human power. 

We are to render unto Caesar what is Caesar’s (Matthew 22:21) -- in other words, we pay taxes, fulfill civic duties, engage in spirited debate, share ideas and generally participate in society and learn lessons from history. We are not called to be desirous or covetous of power, fame, or wealth. We are not directed to follow celebrities, political leaders, or any human authority as our ultimate guide. We are called to follow Christ directly through the Holy Spirit, to be salt and light in the world, and to be advocates for Christ’s teachings in our daily lives. 

Our daily lives are "peer reviewed" by Reality, by our investments, our professional careers, our neighbors, our friends, our family, and even strangers or those we might imagine we will never meet or know. We are to be open to criticism and correction, but we are not to be swayed by the whims of public opinion or the desire for popularity -- the need to be liked is basically idolatrous, i.e. the worship of the potentially inappropriate values of the world. Our accountability is not to the masses or the artificial bots impersonating the masses, but to Christ alone, and we are called to live out our faith authentically and boldly, regardless of the cost.

This is the living reality Christ commands: one body, all accountable to Jesus Christ the Head.
