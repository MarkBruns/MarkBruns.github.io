# My AIservant Faith Operating System (Cato FOS)

[Cato FOS](https://github.com/MarkBruns/CatoFOS) is named after [Inspector Clouseau's manservant, Cato Fong](https://pinkpanther.fandom.com/wiki/Cato_Fong), who Clouseau had trained to attack Clouseau regularly to keep him alert and skilled in the martial arts. I am dead serious about Cato FOS; therefore, I gave the project a comedic name ... because we should never take ourselves so seriously.

This FAITH operational system is a bespoke 50-point life agentic operations agenda ... and part of the practical learning process of ***dogfooding*** agentic tools for the purpose of helping to keeping me on task. 

Cato FOS treats life as a sacred quest under the Holy Trinity, in which I work at being a better steward of time [for a life of worship], own discipline as obedience, see patterns of especially successful organizations, such as a Palantir currently, for the purpose of practically discerning superior intellect, shrewd execution, wisdom, and divine order amid a world full of chaos, deceit and treachery. I have primarily *gone Galt*, so that I can live Stoically while centering everything in constant prayerful communion. The ONLY way to be free and feel good flows out of a faith-aligned lifestyle, ie there is no going to church -- my body must be a temple and I must continually be in state of prayerful worship in order to live.

The system emphasizes:  
- **Vision**: God’s glory and your sanctification.  
- **Discipline**: Perpetual leveling through habit, virtue, and grace.  
- **Time**: Prayerful stewardship, not mere productivity.  
- **Review**: Honest examination before God.  

### 1–10: Foundational Faith & Worldview
1. Anchor every day in the Holy Trinity: Begin with [Trinitarian prayer](#trinitarian-prayer); refactor it, make it your own.  
2. Maintain constant awareness of God’s presence — practice the Presence of God in all tasks (Brother Lawrence style).  
3. View time as a divine, sacred trust: Every moment is a temple loaned by the Creator for His purposes.  
4. Cultivate the most competent, shrewdest mind: Seek and journal divine patterns, providence, and connections in the best examples of daily life.  
5. Embrace Stoic dichotomy of control: Focus energy only on what is yours (faith, virtue, effort); surrender the rest to God.  
6. Articulate and DEFINE your Chief End: Glorify God and be with Him forever — make this the guiding North Star of all goals.  
7. Build identity in Christ alone, not achievements: Regularly affirm “I am a beloved son in whom He is well pleased.”  
8. Practice radical obedience: Treat Scripture commands as non-negotiable operating code.  
9. Integrate faith and reason: Use classical Stoic logic and discernment while submitting both to biblical truth.  
10. Commit to lifelong sanctification: Discipline is worship; leveling up is becoming more like Christ.

### 11–20: Discipline Ownership & Leveling
11. Own your discipline daily: Start each morning with a deliberate “discipline covenant” prayer.  
12. Level discipline weekly: Identify one specific area (e.g., tongue, thoughts, body) to strengthen.  
13. Use Stoic premeditatio malorum: Prayerfully anticipate temptations and pre-decide faithful responses.  
14. Track discipline metrics spiritually: Journal wins, failures, and lessons as confession and thanksgiving.  
15. Build antifragile habits: Intentionally add small frictions that strengthen faith and resolve.  
16. Practice voluntary discomfort: Regular fasting, cold exposure, or silence to train resilience in the Spirit.  
17. Eliminate soul-draining distractions: Ruthlessly audit and cut what weakens prayerful communion.  
18. Celebrate discipline publicly before God only: Avoid performative righteousness (Matthew 6).  
19. Review discipline lapses with grace: Analyze root causes without self-condemnation, then repent and reset.  
20. Pursue excellence as stewardship: Do all things “as unto the Lord” with full effort.

### 21–30: Time Stewardship & Planning
21. Center all planning in prayer: Never set goals without seeking God’s will first.  
22. Structure time around prayer anchors: Morning, midday, evening offices as non-negotiable.  
23. Create “Holy Quests”: Quarterly faith-aligned projects that advance God’s kingdom in your life/calling.  
24. Use weekly “Sabbath Visioning”: Sunday review + prayer for the coming week’s priorities.  
25. Study most interesting companies, apply pattern recognition: Daily tasks and investment connect to divine narrative.  
26. Batch deep work in prayerful focus blocks: Protect 2–3 hours daily for vocation as worship.  
27. Practice single-task communion: Treat each task as an opportunity for ongoing prayer.  
28. Build margin for rest and reflection: Schedule unhurried time with God as essential, not optional.  
29. Steward digital and physical environments: Curate them to support focus on eternal things.  
30. Align calendar with liturgical rhythm: Honor seasons of the Church calendar where possible.

### 31–40: Daily & Weekly Operating Practices
31. Morning Protocol: Scripture, prayer (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication), discipline covenant.  
32. Evening Protocol: Examen (Ignatian style) — Where did I see God? Where did I fail? How will I improve?  
33. Midday Pause: Short prayerful reset and breath prayer (“Lord Jesus Christ, have mercy on me”).  
34. Weekly Review: Sunday audit of faithfulness in time, discipline, relationships, and witness.  
35. Monthly Deep Review: Full day of prayer, fasting, and recalibration of Holy Quests.  
36. Annual Life Audit: Comprehensive examination of growth in virtue, faith, and fruitfulness.  
37. Maintain a “Providence Journal”: Record God’s faithfulness and discerned patterns.  
38. Practice gratitude as discipline: End each day listing 3 specific faith evidences.  
39. Engage in regular confession and accountability: With trusted spiritual brethren.  
40. Serve others as worship: Schedule acts of mercy and kingdom work weekly.

### 41–50: Adaptation, Community & Legacy
41. Stay teachable: Regularly study Scripture, Church Fathers, and Stoic classics through a Christian lens.  
42. Build a personal Rule of Life: Codify your core practices into a living document you revisit.  
43. Foster spiritual friendships: Invest in iron-sharpening-iron relationships.  
44. Measure success by fruit of the Spirit: Not worldly metrics — love, joy, peace, etc.  
45. Prepare for suffering: Stoically accept trials as refining; respond with faith and prayer.  
46. Practice detachment: Hold possessions, status, and plans loosely before God.  
47. Leave a legacy of faithfulness: Mentor, document wisdom, and disciple others.  
48. Iterate the system: Quarterly refine these points based on prayerful discernment.  
49. End each day in surrender: “Into Your hands I commit my spirit” — release control.  
50. Live in eschatological hope: All discipline and stewardship points toward eternity with the Triune God.


### Trinitarian Prayer

Silently fill your mind with contemplates of the Father’s will for Creation, the Son’s practical but mysterious example for human life, the Spirit’s miraculous power to help you realize the Creator's will and to adore the mysteries of Jesus Christ.

*Good morning heavenly Father,
good morning Lord Jesus,
good morning Holy Spirit.

Heavenly Father, I worship you as the creator and sustainer of the universe.
Lord Jesus, I worship you, Savior and Lord of the world.
Holy Spirit, I worship you, sanctifier of the people of God.

Glory to the Father, and to the Son and to the Holy Spirit.

Heavenly Father, I pray that I may live this day in your presence
and please you more and more.

Lord Jesus, I pray that this day I may take up my cross and follow you.

Holy Spirit, I pray that this day you will fill me with yourself and cause your fruit to ripen in my life:
love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control.

Holy, blessed and glorious Trinity, three persons in one God,
have mercy upon me. Amen.*
