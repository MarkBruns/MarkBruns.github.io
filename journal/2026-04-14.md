# Why The Holy Triune God Alone Is Enough

Jesus Himself taught the foundational order of all Scripture when He answered the question about the greatest commandment. In **Matthew 22:37-40** (NIV):

> 37 Jesus replied: “‘Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind.’ 38 This is the first and greatest commandment. 39 And the second is like it: ‘Love your neighbor as yourself.’ 40 All the Law and the Prophets hang on these two commandments.”

Notice the deliberate hierarchy: the command to love the Lord your God with *all* your being is explicitly “the first and greatest.” The second commandment—to love your neighbor as yourself—is “like it” and flows from the first, but it is never equal to or above it. Every other command in Scripture hangs on these two, yet the order is not accidental. The first commandment establishes the root reality; the second is its necessary fruit. When this order is reversed or blurred, idolatry creeps in—whether the idol is a person, a peer group, a church institution, or even well-meaning religious pressure.

### God as the Root and Vine: We Are Branches, Not the Support
The New Testament reinforces this with vivid imagery of dependence on God alone. In **Romans 11:17-24** (NIV), Paul uses the metaphor of an olive tree to describe how believers are grafted in by faith:

> 17 If some of the branches have been broken off, and you, though a wild olive shoot, have been grafted in among the others and now share in the nourishing sap from the olive root, 18 do not consider yourself to be superior to those other branches. If you do, consider this: You do not support the root, but the root supports you. … 20 … you stand by faith. Do not be arrogant, but tremble. … 22 Consider therefore the kindness and sternness of God: sternness to those who fell, but kindness to you, provided that you continue in his kindness. Otherwise, you also will be cut off.

We live by faith alone. Every believer—whether Jew or Gentile, long-time Christian or new—is simply a branch. No branch is superior; no branch supports the tree. The root (the Triune God—Father as source, Son as Redeemer, Spirit as life-giving sap) sustains us all. We are not to dominate one another, nor allow ourselves to be dominated. All other “branches” around us are fellow recipients of grace, not the source of it.

Jesus makes this even more intimate in **John 15:1-8** (NIV):

> 1 “I am the true vine, and my Father is the gardener. … 4 Remain in me, as I also remain in you. No branch can bear fruit by itself; it must remain in the vine. Neither can you bear fruit unless you remain in me.  
> 5 “I am the vine; you are the branches. If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing.”

Here the Holy Triune God is fully revealed: the Father tends the vineyard, the Son *is* the vine itself, and the Holy Spirit is the life-giving presence that produces fruit within us. Apart from this direct, abiding union with Christ, we can do *nothing* of eternal value. The root and vine supply everything; we receive, abide, and bear fruit. This is why we live by faith alone—faith in the God who grafts, sustains, and prunes.

### The Triune God Supplies Everything We Need—Directly
Because God is the root and vine, He is utterly sufficient. He does not merely point us to secondary sources; He *is* the source. Consider these clear promises:

- **Provision for every need**: “And my God will meet all your needs according to the riches of his glory in Christ Jesus” (**Philippians 4:19**, NIV).  
- **Sufficiency in all things**: “And God is able to bless you abundantly, so that in all things at all times, having all that you need, you will abound in every good work” (**2 Corinthians 9:8**); “Not that we are competent in ourselves to claim anything for ourselves, but our competence comes from God” (**2 Corinthians 3:5**).  
- **Seek Him first**: “But seek first his kingdom and his righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well” (**Matthew 6:33**).  
- **Complete in Christ**: “and in Christ you have been brought to fullness” (**Colossians 2:10**).

He addresses our pains and fears (Psalm 34:18; 2 Corinthians 1:3-4), forgives us when we confess (1 John 1:9), and gives us the grace to forgive others (Matthew 6:14-15). In the Lord’s Prayer itself (Matthew 6:9-13), Jesus teaches us to look *directly* to the Father for daily bread, forgiveness, and deliverance from temptation and evil. The Holy Spirit leads us away from temptation and comforts us (John 14:16; Galatians 5:16). We are never truly alone: “Never will I leave you; never will I forsake you” (**Hebrews 13:5**; see also Deuteronomy 31:6).

Yes, God blesses us with human companionship—friends, spouses, fellow believers, and the local church. Fellowship is commanded and vital (**Hebrews 10:24-25**). But these are *gifts from the root*, not the root itself. The church is the body whose Head is Christ (**Ephesians 5:23**); it is not a substitute mediator. Scripture is crystal clear: “For there is one God and one mediator between God and mankind, the man Christ Jesus” (**1 Timothy 2:5**, NIV). No institution, pastor, denomination, or peer group stands between you and the Triune God.

### Why Nothing—Church, Peers, or Pressure—Can Be Allowed Between Us and God
The first commandment’s supremacy protects us from idolatry in all its subtle forms. Exodus 20:3-5 forbids any other “gods” before Him. Trusting ultimately in human beings or institutions is spiritually dangerous:

- “Fear of man will prove to be a snare, but whoever trusts in the Lord is kept safe” (**Proverbs 29:25**, NIV).  
- “Do not put your trust in princes, in human beings, who cannot save” (**Psalm 146:3**).  
- “This is what the Lord says: ‘Cursed is the one who trusts in man…’” (**Jeremiah 17:5**).

Even religious structures can become idols—think of those in Jeremiah 7:4 who trusted in “the temple of the Lord” as a talisman rather than in the Lord Himself. Peer pressure, church politics, or the desire for approval can never be allowed to eclipse direct obedience to God. We are commanded to love, respect, and serve others (the second commandment), but that love must flow *out of* our primary devotion to God, never compete with it. We trust others in a measured, secondary way; we trust, fear, worship, and adore the Triune God with *all* our heart, soul, and mind.

This is freedom: no branch needs to dominate or be dominated. Every believer stands directly connected to the Root by faith. The Holy Triune God—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—meets every need, forgives every sin, supplies every grace, and produces every fruit. Loving Him first is not narrow; it is the only way to love others rightly, without superiority, without fear, and without compromise. When God is first, everything else falls into its proper, blessed, secondary place. This is why Jesus is enough—because in the Triune God we have everything. Abide in Him, and you will bear much fruit.
