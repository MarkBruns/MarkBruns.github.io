# Pareto Principle—LEADERSHIP -- FIRST Heal the Rot Within

The Pareto principle is not some sort of suggested wild idea; the 20/80 rule is a long-proven law of effective action. FOCUSING first on the most consequential twenty percent of problems to FIRST generate eighty percent of results, which provide for greater ability to then reassess and re-Pareto and focus on next twenty percent to continue. For the Catholic Church in our age, that decisive twenty percent consists of the self-inflicted wounds that have shattered credibility, drained resources, and crippled moral authority: the lingering clerical abuse crisis and the politicization of the pulpit. These things are fully within the Church's control. Every other crisis—persecution in Nigeria, doctrinal confusion, evangelization—multiplies or collapses in direct proportion to whether we first close these wounds.

Ignore Pareto principle and the consequence is predictable for any large organization ... scarce goodwill is squandered by hysterical leadership, inept problem-solving, and then the inept leaders fear-monger and preach to distract attention from the very chaos their distractive frittering away of resources on politicking has created.

**Internal betrayal is the multiplier.** The documented record is unambiguous: thousands of victims across decades, systematic reassignment of credibly accused priests, billions in settlements, collapsing vocations and donations. Reforms have been enacted, yet survivor groups, independent commissions, and the Church’s own data show the work remains unfinished. Legacy damage persists because trust is not restored by one summit or one motu proprio; it is rebuilt through decades of visible, uniform, zero-tolerance discipline—full transparency, immediate laicization, and ironclad accountability for every bishop who shields the guilty. Anything less is not mercy; it is institutional self-deception.

Politicization compounds the hemorrhage. The Church exists to form souls in relationship with God, not to serve as a get-out-the-vote machine or a platform for public virtue-signaling. Subsidiarity and the narrow limits of papal infallibility are not obscure technicalities; they are firewalls. When Rome issues guidance that reads as electoral direction—on abortion, migration, or any prudential contest—it does not enlighten consciences; it alienates the faithful, invites cynicism, and diverts the papal megaphone from the very crises that demand its full weight. Catholics are adults. They must learn to apply unchanging moral principles in a democracy through prayer, Scripture, and local formation—not through directives from the Apostolic Palace.

A healed Church would speak with exponential force on external horrors. Nigeria is not a footnote. Open Doors and USCIRF data for 2024–2025 record 3,490 Christian murders there—72 percent of the global total—part of a cumulative toll exceeding 50,000 dead and 18,000 churches and schools destroyed since 2009. This is sustained, disproportionate targeting by jihadist ideology and radicalized actors, not generic “farmer-herder conflict.” A Church whose own house is in order can demand global attention without the suspicion of selective outrage or moral exhaustion. Credibility is leverage; credibility lost is leverage squandered.

Bureaucratic inertia, clericalism, global diversity of threats, diplomatic caution, and fear of further scandal are real. They are also irrelevant to the Pareto calculation. The internal crisis is controllable; the external one is not. Every resource, every headline, every ounce of goodwill spent on political theater or on papering over abuse is stolen from the persecuted, from the next generation of priests, and from the Church’s eternal mission.

The necessary path is clear, and necessarily a hard path, but it is the only path to true renewal:

- **Stop the politicization.** Cease all appearance of electoral engineering. Instead teach the principles of conscientious problem-solving and right judgment in a noisy world; foster the independence, conscience, and spiritual discernment of the laity so they can know whom and what to trust.

- **Finish the purge of systematic pedophilia.** Implement and sustain zero-tolerance globally and visibly—no exceptions, no quiet reassignments justified by priest shortages or any other excuse. There can be no mercy that excuses justice. The effort must be maintained for decades, then perpetually.

- **Rebuild trust at the Root.** The Church and the body of believers are not the root; they are the vine. Return the Church to its true center: the Holy Triune God, adored and imitated in Christ. The personal, daily quest for God and the strengthening of souls is a matter of *being* the change—not agitating for politicians or chasing cultural influence and political favor.

- **Then speak.** With restored moral authority and renewed clarity, turn the full weight of the Church’s voice against the slaughter in Nigeria and every place where Christians are martyred for the faith. Call those who are moved to fight: go to the ground, confront the violence, build reconciliation person-to-person. Be the Church’s hands and feet in the world. The Church’s voice is not a weapon; it is a beacon. It can illuminate the darkness only if it is not itself the source of shadow.

The rot is ours to excise. The hour is late. The harvest is great, the laborers few—and they will remain few until we stop hemorrhaging them. Heal the internal wound first, decisively and without distraction. Everything else follows.
