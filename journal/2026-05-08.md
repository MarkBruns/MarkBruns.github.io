# Starter List Of 200 Best Niche Microenterprise Business Ideas

Each is explicitly designed as a solo or 1-2 person problem-solving business that fits [Ali Abdaal’s $100K roadmap](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XIhc7_Ptrpk). Every idea centers on **prioritization and focus on what matters most** — Pareto 80/20 ruthlessness, radical simplification, minimalism + extreme ownership (Jocko-style), fasting as a focus weapon or separating sieve, ditching bad habits/substance abuse, political disruption (Trump-style tariff renegotiation), DOGE-style government efficiency, or forward-deployed DoD contracting (Palantir/Anduril model of saving trillions by fixing the ugly bits no one wants to touch). These are microenterprise ideas -- they are intended to be high-margin ($2K–$10K+ per client), low-overhead, skill-leveraged, and built around the unpleasant truths that deliver outsized results.


These ideas are deliberately built for fast validation (discovery calls in the niches people hate thinking about) and high-margin delivery. 

The point is pick niches one that really matches your craft skills. Your own starter list WILL look different given your skills.

The point of this list is to help me think about how I solve a painful, high-leverage “shit sandwich” problem that most people spend a lot of time/$ trying to actively avoid.  

Of course, I don't need 200 ideas -- I only really need 1 great idea, but I might need something like 5 - 25 different ways to try. It would be best to have 5 knockout ideas, but it's probably best to think in terms of 25 things, because my perception will not match other people's reality. 

As I go through the sorting, sifting, sieving process, it'll be gest to combine ideas, refactor concepts and generate new hopefully viable niche ideas.

In order to do this I will use the following A-D: sorting process:

A) Solid candidate for one of my first things to try ... I am hoping to get 25, need 5 good things, but if I don't have even one, it's best to start over with a new starter list.
B) Close, not yet cigar material -- maybe if something changes or an opportunities arises ... hoping to get 40 of the ALMOST B-level ideas.
C) Interesting; probably an idea worth riffing on, but different things about it would have to change ... I am guessing that there are likely around 60 in this list.
D) Parking lot or Area 51, unapplicable, unidentifiable flying object that probably should be tossed ... probably at least 75

Run the 6 P’s from [Ali’s video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XIhc7_Ptrpk) to get a one-page offer ... [Alex Hormozi $100M Offers](https://www.audible.com/pd/100M-Offers-Audiobook/B09BK57L5W)

* Person -- WHO is the center of the bullseye in your target market?
* Problem -- WHAT is the problem and what is the PAIN that they have?
* Promise -- How will we viablly assure that we fix the person's problem?
* Plan -- Sketch out transformation roadmap for how this promise will be achieved
* Product -- Everything about the product that will actualize the plan
* Price -- Come up with a price that is too good to refuse, ie people will REFUSE a free offer because they assume it's not a product

The uglier the problem, the more they’ll pay.
