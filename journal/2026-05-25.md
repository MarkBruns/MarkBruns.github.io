# 120 Pomodoros Per Week -- Discipline SMART Goal For Summer

This 120 Pomodoros/week structure should be sustainable. It allows for 10 30 minute breaks everyday -- going for a walk is a break; taking a power nap is a break; fixing/eating a sandwich is a break. 

Getting 120 Pomodoros of focused work in a week might seem somewhat demanding, but the point is building the discipline of repeatable habits. It's not exactly a new approach for me -- it's just sort of doubling down on what has worked, with self-improving cognitive-rewiring aligned with the classics from scripture and millenia of stoic philosophy, horatio alger stories and success literature ... it's not really anything new, but I also know from experience that doing this IS not easy and does not just happen, ie it requires a constant mental PUSH.

## Aim to 2X as much accomplishment in 1 week.

Schedule the week into **two mini-weeks**:

- **Mini-Week 1 (Mon–Tue–Wed)**: High-momentum start → execution → deep discipline + thorough 5S of tech debt.

- **Mini-Week 2 (Thu–Fri–Sat)**: Continued execution → momentum finishing or close → final house-cleaning of backlogged ToDos and setting stage for next week.

- **Sunday**: Full Sabbath (rest, reflection, extended Holy practices ... a solid BREAK that is not polluted by any one else's priorities).

## All Days Have Core RULES:

- Rise at 4:00 AM
- 4:00–5:00 AM Holy Hour (contemplation, adoration, prayer, meditation, gratitude journaling, biblestudy, biblical exposition)
- 15 hours = 30 X 30-minute blocks (25 min focused Pomodoro + 4 min HIIT/mobility/stretch + 1 min refocus)
- ~20 FOCUSED blocks + ~10 RECOVERY blocks (meals, walks, naps, buffers) from ~5:00 AM–8:00 PM
- Severely minimized social (only scheduled social media batches if needed)
- Bed by 8:30–9:00 PM

## Daily Schedule Assignment & Flow

**Monday: Riff 2 – Momentum Builder** (Hit the ground running – Big Wins Day)  
Best day for high-leverage, important tasks. Progressive intensity to create early-week momentum.  
- Morning blocks (5–11 AM): 10 blocks on highest-priority soul + income/creation projects.  
- Afternoon: Apply big wins with strong energy.  
- Focus: Set tone for all days, ie always work at getting the hardest, most rewarding items done early, to sustain accomplishment momentum.

**Tuesday: Riff 1 – Soul-First Fortress** (Deep Spiritual Alignment + Consistency)  
Solid execution day with strong spiritual grounding after Monday’s big push on hardest things.  
- Heavy emphasis on soul-aligned, deeper, longer-term work.  
- Use the strict structure to maintain discipline; embrace the suck of structure.
- Lift and use endurance pushing breaks / lifting exercise to rebuild cheerfulness.

**Wednesday: Riff 6 – Meta-Discipline Architect** (Discipline + House-Cleaning Nasty Tasks)  
Your 10,000-foot coaching day + tackling anything that slipped Monday/Tuesday.  
- **4:00–5:00 AM Holy Hour**: Extended meta-review of Mon–Tue.  
- **Blocks 1–8 (5–9 AM)**: Full weekly audit + scoring of previous riffs. Identify unfinished/nasty tasks.  
- **Blocks 9–16 (9 AM–1 PM)**: Ruthlessly attack the nastiest lingering tasks (the ones you’ve been avoiding).  
- **Blocks 17–20 (1–8 PM)**: Jail Ministry; plan Mini-Week 2 (Thu–Sat), set keystone experiments, and light execution.  
- Set tone for week with recovery blocks: Use walks for reflective thinking and gratitude for tough tasks.  
This day resets discipline, cleans up, prevents loose ends from carrying over.

**Thursday: Riff 4 – Cheerful Rhythm** (Varied Energy & Joy)  
- Fresh start to the second mini-week with upbeat, sustainable energy; like Monday
- Thursday is about doubling-down and setting a positive tone to finish the mini-week.  
- Alternate focus and movement blocks as needed to stay fresh.  
- Insert mobility exercise and stretching joy blocks to keep attitude high mid-week.  
- Good day for creative weed-pulling, transplanting or relationship-with-Creation work.

**Friday: Riff 5 – Sustainable Long Game** (Flexibility + Recovery Focus)  
Balanced execution with built-in adaptability as the week winds down.  
- Use energy-based heavy-lifting exercise flex blocks.  
- Emphasize longer walks, heavy lifting endurance and sustainable pacing.  
- Lifting prepares high-quality output before the weekend clean-up.

**Saturday: Riff 3 – Removal-First Minimalist** (Final Clean-Up & Set Stage for Next Week)  
Mirrors Wednesday’s clean-up role but lighter — finish loose ends and remove distractions.  
- Morning: Complete any remaining nasty or unfinished tasks from the week.  
- Mid-day: Strong removal focus (declutter digital/physical, unsubscribe, delete, simplify systems).  
- Afternoon: Light planning blocks for next Monday (prep clothes, priorities, victory log).  
- Finish with heavy-bag to relieve stress or tightly scope a mini-project to put a dent in last extra optional-if-finished bonus thing.

**Sunday: Sabbath – Full Rest & Reflection**  
No Pomodoro blocks. No structured work.  
- Extended Holy Hour(s) throughout the day.  
- Gentle walks, classics reading, prayer, gratitude, adoration, unapologetic solitude.  
- Light reflection on the week using gentle Meta questions without scoring pressure.  
- Read, think, prepare soul and mind for Monday’s strong start.

## Weekly Flow Supports Long-Term Discipline

- **Two Mini-Weeks** give you psychological freshness and two natural “reset points” (Wednesday Meta-discipline day + Saturday clean-up).  
- Monday big-win energy carries into Wednesday’s discipline audit.  
- Saturday removal focus clears the decks completely before Sabbath ... and if all the work's done early, then put a dent in something optional  
- The rotation prevents boredom while the Meta Day (Wednesday) constantly improves the system.  
- Built-in grit: Nasty tasks are deliberately funneled to Wednesday and Saturday — this trains delayed gratification and cheerful endurance.

## Daily Non-Negotiables For All Days**:
- Start every Holy Hour with gratitude that drives joyous “NOWness” and looking at life from Creator's perspective
- End every workday (except Sabbath) with a quick cheerful victory log: “What has gone well day before? What problems/opportunities are out there? What am I grateful for? What is one adjustment I need to make today for setting stage for better tomorrows.”
- If a block is missed: Immediately restart with a 30-second upbeat recentering/gratitude prayer instead of guilt ... so what? Just do your best from now on ... if you can STICK this schedule, there are 20 focused pomodoros per day and 120 per a week.
- Track weekly in a simple Examen Ledger during Wednesday Meta discipline building day ... keep the tracking simple; don't allow it to become tracking for the sake of tracking.
