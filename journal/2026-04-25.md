# UAVs, UGVs, UUVs, Hybrids, Swarms

The list emphasizes **biomimetic designs** where possible (mimicking animals for efficiency, stealth, agility, or multi-modal movement) but includes variety for coverage. Many research prototypes are not for sale but have papers, code, or build instructions online; commercial ones can be purchased or kits ordered. Entries include a brief description, primary developer/institution/company, and a direct public URL for more details, specs, papers, purchase, or building (e.g., GitHub for open-source).

### Aerial Biomimetic (Bird/Insect/Bat-Inspired Ornithopters, Flappers, and Micro-Drones)
These mimic flapping wings, perching, or insect agility for quiet, agile flight.

1. **BionicBird Swift** — Smartphone-controlled flapping-wing ornithopter drone that replicates real bird flight (no propellers), reaches 31 km/h, stable at low speeds. Developer: Bionic Bird (France). URL: https://www.bionicbird.com/world/theswift_details/ (purchase available).

2. **MetaBird** — Biomimetic RC bird drone with natural flapping and gliding. Developer: Bionic Bird. URL: https://www.bionicbird.com/world/shop/metabird/.

3. **X-Fly Ornithopter** — Sensor-assisted flapping drone with autopilot for bird-like flight. Developer: Bionic Bird. URL: https://www.bionicbird.com/ (commercial kits).

4. **RoboBee** — Tiny insect-scale flapping microrobot (~80 mg) that hovers, steers, and can swim/fly/perch using piezoelectric "artificial muscles." Developer: Harvard Wyss Institute (Robert Wood lab). URL: https://wyss.harvard.edu/technology/robobees-autonomous-flying-microrobots/ (research prototypes; Pop-Up MEMS fabrication details).

5. **DelFly Nimble** — Agile tailless flapping-wing MAV that hovers and flies in any direction like an insect. Developer: TU Delft Micro Air Vehicle Lab. URL: https://www.delfly.nl/.

6. **DelFly II** — Earlier four-winged flapping MAV for stable forward flight and obstacle resilience. Developer: TU Delft. URL: https://www.delfly.nl/history2018/.

7. **SmartBird** — Lightweight ornithopter mimicking herring gull (autonomous takeoff, flight, landing via wing twist). Developer: Festo Bionic Learning Network. URL: https://www.festo.com/us/en/e/about-festo/research-and-development/bionic-learning-network/bionic-flying-objects/smartbird-id_33686/.

8. **BionicOpter** — Dragonfly-inspired ultralight flapping drone that hovers, glides, and maneuvers in all directions. Developer: Festo. URL: https://www.festo.com/us/en/e/about-festo/research-and-development/bionic-learning-network/bionic-flying-objects/bionicopter-id_33493/.

9. **BionicSwift** — Flock of agile artificial swifts with indoor GPS for collective collision-free flight. Developer: Festo. URL: https://www.festo.com/us/en/e/about-festo/research-and-development/bionic-learning-network/bionic-flying-objects/bionicswift-id_326830/.

10. **eMotionButterflies** — Ultralight flapping butterfly drones with coordinated swarm behavior via indoor GPS. Developer: Festo. URL: https://stem.festo.com/us/en/our-approach/bionics/bionic-projects-by-festo/index.html.

11. **BionicFlyingFox** — Bat-inspired membrane-wing flapping drone for efficient, low-energy flight. Developer: Festo. URL: https://stem.festo.com/us/en/our-approach/bionics/bionic-projects-by-festo/index.html.

12. **Nano Hummingbird** — Hummingbird-mimicking micro air vehicle with flapping wings for hovering and directional flight. Developer: AeroVironment. URL: https://spectrum.ieee.org/aerovironments-nano-hummingbird-surveillance-bot-would-probably-fool-you (research/demo info).

13. **PigeonBot** — Biohybrid drone with real pigeon feathers for morphing wings and realistic flight. Developer: Stanford University (BDML lab). URL: https://bdml.stanford.edu/pmwiki/uploads/Main/PerchingPublications/Hoffmann-J-Comp-Matls2022.pdf (paper with design details).

14. **SNAG (Stereotyped Nature-Inspired Aerial Grasper)** — Falcon-inspired perching/grasping legs for drones to latch onto branches. Developer: Stanford University. URL: https://news.stanford.edu/press-releases/2021/12/01/bird-like-robot-perches-grasps/.

15. **LisHawk** — Goshawk-inspired morphing-wing drone that adjusts wing/tail area for agile flight. Developer: EPFL (Switzerland). URL: Research papers via https://www.researchgate.net/figure/Classification-of-biomimetic-drones-according-to-weight (linked studies).

16. **KUBeetle-S** — Insect-inspired tailless flapping-wing micro air vehicle with independent wing control. Developer: Konkuk University (Korea). URL: Multiple papers; see https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2214574520301176.

17. **Bat Bot B2** — Bat-morphing flapping-wing robot with stretchable silicone wings. Developer: Caltech. URL: https://robotics.sciencemag.org/content/2/3/eaal2505.

## Drone Swarm Intelligence.

The following cover multi-agent coordination, formation control, collision avoidance, reinforcement learning (RL), consensus algorithms, localization, trajectory optimization, simulators, and hardware integration for UAV swarms (primarily quadcopters/Crazyflie/PX4-based). All are publicly available on GitHub or linked open repositories—no proprietary or classified projects.

Each entry includes a brief description (emphasizing swarm intelligence aspects like decentralized control, flocking, or multi-agent algorithms), primary developer/institution, and the direct URL for code, documentation, building, or simulation setup. Many include ROS/ROS2, Gazebo/AirSim/PyBullet support for easy replication or hardware deployment.

### Major Swarm Frameworks & Hardware Platforms
61. **Crazyswarm2** — ROS 2 stack for large-scale Crazyflie nano-quadcopter swarms enabling decentralized formation flying, trajectory tracking, and multi-agent coordination. Developer: IMRCLab (KU Leuven). URL: https://github.com/IMRCLab/crazyswarm2

62. **Crazyswarm** — Original large nano-quadcopter swarm control framework (now legacy; use v2 for new projects). Developer: USC ACTLab. URL: https://github.com/USC-ACTLab/crazyswarm

63. **CoFlyers** — General platform for collective flying of swarm drones with decentralized control and coordination algorithms. Developer: micros-uav. URL: https://github.com/micros-uav/CoFlyers

64. **Skybrush Server** — Open-core backend for drone swarm management, light shows, and multi-UAV coordination. Developer: skybrush-io. URL: https://github.com/skybrush-io/skybrush-server

65. **Skybrush Live** — Ground control station GUI frontend for real-time drone swarm operations and shows. Developer: skybrush-io. URL: https://github.com/skybrush-io/live

66. **ORCUS** — Autonomous swarm kamikaze/engagement system with AI target detection, task allocation, and collision-safe coordination (ArduPilot/ROS/Gazebo). Developer: koesan. URL: https://github.com/koesan/ORCUS
